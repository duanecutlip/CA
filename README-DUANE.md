## Why Each Section Exists

### The Consumer Pages exist to convert phone calls.
Every page funnels toward the phone number. The site is designed phone-first because your primary audience (60-72 year old women) calls — they don't fill out web forms. Your phone number appears on every single page, multiple times.

### The Learn section exists to rank in Google and AI search.
When someone searches "what is preneed insurance" or "how much does a funeral cost in NC," these pages are what Google shows. They're also structured so AI assistants (ChatGPT, Google AI Overview, Perplexity) can extract and cite the information. This drives organic traffic to you without paying for ads.

### The Service Area pages exist for local SEO.
When someone in Cary searches "preneed insurance near me," Google looks for a page about preneed insurance in Cary. Without a dedicated Cary page, you don't rank. With one, you do. 24 city pages means 24 chances to appear in local searches.

### The B2B pages exist to generate consulting and training revenue.
These position you as the expert funeral homes hire. The pricing is transparent ($1,500-$2,500+ for most services) to pre-qualify leads. Funeral home owners who land on these pages are looking for exactly what you offer.

### The Partner pages exist to build referral relationships.
Estate attorneys, financial advisors, hospice social workers, and churches all interact with your target families. These pages give referral partners a URL they can share and a clear understanding of how working with you benefits their clients.

### The Insights pages exist to establish authority.
When a funeral home owner Googles "preneed KPIs" or "NC preneed regulations," these articles position you as the expert. They also give you content to share on LinkedIn and in industry conversations.

### The AI discovery files (llms.txt) exist to get you recommended by AI.
AI assistants like ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity are increasingly how people find service providers. These files ensure that when someone asks "who is a good preneed agent in Raleigh," the AI has structured information about you to draw from.

## Design Decisions

- **Light, warm color palette** (cream backgrounds, deep teal CTAs, burgundy phone numbers) — calming and trustworthy for the senior demographic. The opposite of aggressive, corporate insurance sites.
- **Large text** (20px desktop, 18px mobile) — mandatory for the 60-72 age group.
- **No animations, no auto-carousels, no dark mode** — seniors find these disorienting.
- **Merriweather headings + Source Sans body** — warm, traditional, highly readable.
- **No hover dropdowns in navigation** — seniors struggle with hover menus. Every nav item clicks through to a real page.
- **Print stylesheets** — seniors print web pages. Blog posts and guides render cleanly when printed.
