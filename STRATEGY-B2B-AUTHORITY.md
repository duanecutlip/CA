## 4. Industry Knowledge Duane Should Showcase

### Regulatory Expertise (The "Moat")

**North Carolina (N.C.G.S. 90-210.60 through 90-210.73):**
- Two contract types: Standard and Inflation-Proof
- Trust fund requirements and Board oversight
- $20 per-contract Board fee and reporting requirements
- Board-prescribed contract forms (oral contracts prohibited)
- Recovery Fund for consumer protection
- Preneed licensee obligations and examination procedures
- SL 2022-63 (SB 138) — recent legislative updates

**South Carolina (SC Code 32-7-10 through 32-7-120):**
- Licensing through SC Department of Consumer Affairs
- $250 one-time license fee + security deposit
- Trust account or burial insurance policy requirements
- Trust fund encumbrance prohibitions
- SC Board of Funeral Service oversight (SCLLR)
- Enforcement and investigation of unlicensed providers

**Federal:**
- FTC Funeral Rule (16 CFR Part 453) compliance
- General Price List (GPL) requirements
- Casket Rule and third-party merchandise
- Cooling-off period regulations

### Preneed Program KPIs Duane Should Teach

| KPI | What It Measures | Benchmark |
|---|---|---|
| **Preneed Penetration Rate** | Ratio of preneed contracts to at-need cases | Top performers: 1.5-2.0x at-need volume |
| **Average Contract Value (ACV)** | Average face amount per preneed policy | $3,000-$7,000 depending on market |
| **Lead Source ROI** | Cost per lead and conversion by channel | Track: direct mail, digital, seminars, referrals |
| **Conversion Rate** | Leads to signed contracts | 25-40% is strong |
| **Cancellation Rate** | Policies terminated before maturity | Below 5% is healthy |
| **Preneed Revenue Contribution** | % of total revenue from preneed | Established programs: 10-15%+ of annual revenue |
| **At-Need Capture Rate** | % of preneed families who use the funeral home at-need | 85%+ indicates strong program |
| **Counselor Productivity** | Policies written per counselor per month | 8-15 policies/month per full-time counselor |
